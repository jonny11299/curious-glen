On Lemon Grove: the case was *Roberto Alvarez v. Board of Trustees of the Lemon Grove School District*, decided in California Superior Court in 1931. Because it was a state court ruling — not federal — it couldn't set national precedent. The school board tried to build a separate school for Mexican children and called it an "Americanization" program. The community organized, kept their kids home, and won.

It was essentially rediscovered in 1985 through a PBS documentary called *The Lemon Grove Incident*. There's a historical marker in Lemon Grove now. The Smithsonian has acknowledged it. But it's not in most textbooks.
